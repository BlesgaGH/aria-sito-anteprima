Research projects and collaborations

We take part in national and international research projects on safety, risk analysis, the environment and human factors.

Associated partner · 2021–2025

CISC

Marie Skłodowska-Curie ITN · Horizon 2020 (EU)

Collaborative Intelligence for Safety Critical Systems: an international training network (ITN) for a new generation of researchers in collaborative intelligence, at the crossroads of artificial intelligence, human factors, neuroergonomics and systems safety engineering. The project trained 14 PhD candidates in developing human-in-the-loop AI systems for safety-critical applications — from manufacturing to the IoT and critical infrastructure. ARIA contributed as associated partner.

  • Partners: TU Dublin (lead), Politecnico di Torino, Università degli Studi di Milano, University of Kragujevac, IVECO, Irish Manufacturing Research, Pilz, Adient, Mathema, mBrainTrain, HUGIN Expert, Software Competence Center Hagenberg, European DIGITAL SME Alliance
Project website →
Lead partner · 2020–2023

SALS

Finpiemonte · POR-FESR 2014/2020 – Innovation Clusters

Food-safety management along the supply chain with advanced tools: innovative sensors and real-time data-sharing networks to extend control across the entire chain.

  • Partners: Santer Reply, La Gentile s.r.l., Politecnico di Torino, Università degli Studi di Torino
Project website →
Partner · 2020–2021

SAFELIVERY

EIT-FOOD · Crisis Response Initiative (EU)

A safer food-delivery service during and after the COVID-19 pandemic. ARIA developed HACCP procedures integrated with human factors and delivery conditions, together with a prototype tamper-proof box with a smart lock.

  • Partners: Università di Bologna (lead), Queen's University Belfast, TU Dublin, Tecnoalimenti S.C.p.A.
Project website →
Partner · 2011–2016

INNHF

Innovation through Human Factors in Risk Analysis and Management

Integration of human and managerial factors and maintenance actions into traditional risk-analysis methodologies. ARIA hosted a researcher for three years, focused on human factors in ATEX procedures.

  • Partners: FCA, Pfizer, Tetrapak, Politecnico di Torino, Politecnico di Milano, Trinity College Dublin, University of Kragujevac (Serbia)
Partner · 2014–2015

GEOMAT

Regione Piemonte · POR FESR 07/13

Geopolymer binders as an alternative to Portland cement. ARIA carried out preliminary Life Cycle Analysis studies for the technical and economic assessment of the product.

  • Partners: Buzzi Unicem, Politecnico di Torino
Partner · 2012–2014

DUALCEM

Regione Piemonte · POR FESR 07/13

Development of high-durability self-healing cementitious materials without compromising their structural properties. ARIA provided analysis in support of the development work and a preliminary Life Cycle Analysis.

  • Partners: Buzzi Unicem, Politecnico di Torino
Agreement · 2012–2019

ASSOGALVANICA

Consulting support for members

Support on compliance with Italian Legislative Decrees 81/2008 and 105/2015, with a weekly telephone information service and a free preliminary consultation for members.
